Quasicrystal plotter

Order without periodicity, from nothing but interfering plane waves.

Each pixel sums N cosine waves whose wavevectors are spread evenly over half a circle. For N = 2 or 3 you get an ordinary periodic lattice — but for N = 5 the pattern shows tenfold rotational symmetry, which no periodic crystal can have. This is the same order found in Penrose tilings, and it is exactly how quasicrystalline optical lattices are made in cold-atom labs: superimpose laser beams at the right angles and the atoms feel a quasiperiodic potential.
